The Blockchain

How Minima structures its chain for mobile-scale pruning

Structure

The Minima blockchain has two parts:

  • TxPoW Tree — the most recent ~1024 blocks, kept in full
  • Cascading Chain — an unbroken chain of "Super Block" headers proving cumulative Proof-of-Work without storing every block

The root of the TxPoW Tree connects to the tip of the Cascading Chain.

The Cascading Chain

This is Minima's solution to proving chain weight without keeping all blocks. Key properties:

  • 32 levels (0-31), maximum 128 blocks per level
  • Each level is 2x harder than the previous — grows logarithmically
  • A "Super Block" is any block that by chance exceeds the difficulty of its level
  • Block weight at level L = base_weight × 2L

Cascade Updates

Every 100 blocks, the cascade is updated:

  1. The first 100 blocks from the main chain are added to the cascade
  2. Working backwards, blocks that don't meet the next level's difficulty are pruned
  3. CoinProofs for unspent coins are preserved in the new root's MMR Set
  4. The remaining ~1024 blocks stay unpruned on the main chain

Super Parents

Every block header contains references to its parent at every existing Super level in the cascade. This creates an immutable link structure connecting all Super Blocks across all levels — ensuring the cascade is unbroken and independently verifiable.
